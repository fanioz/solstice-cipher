# Level Progression Game Design Document

## 1. Overview
Solstice Cipher is a light-routing puzzle game where players position optical tools (mirrors, splitters, benders, portals) from a limited inventory ("Briefcase") onto a 2D board to guide light beams into target letter glyphs, spelling out celestial-themed words.

The game is structured into **100 levels** split across **10 tiers** of 10 levels each. Each tier introduces a new tool, increases word length, and tightens the piece budget. The first 15 levels are hand-crafted tutorial/introductory challenges; levels 16 to 100 are procedurally generated.

---

## 2. Tools and Mechanics
The game features 7 distinct optical components:
1. **Mirror** (Tier 1): Reflects beams at 90 degrees. Snaps to 15-degree increments.
2. **Prism / Splitter** (Tier 2): Splits an incoming beam into a 90-degree reflection and a straight transmission.
3. **Filter** (Tier 3): Tints a beam a specific color (Red, Green, Blue). Target glyphs must receive matching color light.
4. **Shade** (Tier 4): Blocks light beams entirely. Used to block incorrect routes.
5. **Bender** (Tier 5): Deflects beams at 45 degrees. Snaps to 15-degree increments.
6. **Portal** (Tier 6): Transports beams bidirectionally between paired portal locations.
7. **Combiner** (Tier 7): Emits an output beam only when two separate input beams strike it simultaneously.

## 4. Difficulty Flow (Mini-Arc)
Each 10-level tier follows a difficulty mini-arc:
- **X1–X3 (Intro)**: Introduces the new tool in isolation.
- **X4–X6 (Combine)**: Combines the tool with prior mechanics.
- **X7–X9 (Challenge)**: Tighter budgets, multiple obstacles.
- **X0 (Boss)**: The ultimate test of the tier's mechanics with minimal budget.

---

## 5. Procedural Generation Guidelines
Levels 16 to 100 are procedurally generated using the `LevelScaffolder` and `BackwardsSolver` systems:
1. **Word Selection**: Selects a word from the thematic curated database matching the target length range.
2. **Source/Sun Placement**: Placed at a fixed position `Vector2i(6, 16)` representing `(390, 990)` pixels. This center-aligned bottom socket is protected from hexagonal and octagonal corner cuts.
3. **Glyph Placement**: Letter glyphs are placed randomly on the grid with overlap checks and distance checks.
4. **Solving Backwards**: A pathfinder traces paths back from the glyphs to the source, placing required mirrors, splitters, or filters dynamically along continuous 15-degree ray paths (ADR-0005). Grid cell coordinates serve as visual alignment guidelines rather than discrete coordinate limits.
5. **Validation**: Rejects layouts exceeding the tier's piece budget or computational limits.

## 7. Puzzle Piece Distribution Formulas (Tiers 2–10)

To support procedural variety while ensuring readability, briefcase inventories are generated by combining the level's physical solution set $S$ ($|S| = k_{\text{sol}}$) with a procedurally generated padding set $P$ of distractor tools.

### Briefcase Budget Allocation ($N_B$)
The total capacity of the briefcase $N_B(L)$ for level $L = 10(T-1) + k$ (where $T$ is the Tier and $k \in [1,10]$ is the level's position in the tier) is defined by the mini-arc difficulty cadence:
- **Intro (Levels X1–X3)**: $N_B(L) = \text{Budget}_{\min}(T)$
- **Combine (Levels X4–X6)**: $N_B(L) = \text{Budget}_{\min}(T) + 1$
- **Challenge (Levels X7–X9)**: $N_B(L) = \text{Budget}_{\max}(T)$
- **Boss (Level X0)**: $N_B(L) = \text{Budget}_{\max}(T) - 1$

Where the tier budgets are defined as:
- **Tiers 2–6**: $\text{Budget}_{\min} = T + 1$, $\text{Budget}_{\max} = T + 2$
- **Tier 7 (Convergence)**: $\text{Budget}_{\min} = 6$, $\text{Budget}_{\max} = 8$
- **Tier 8 (Mastery)**: $\text{Budget}_{\min} = 7$, $\text{Budget}_{\max} = 9$
- **Tier 9 (Cipher)**: $\text{Budget}_{\min} = 8$, $\text{Budget}_{\max} = 10$
- **Tier 10 (Solstice)**: $\text{Budget}_{\min} = 8$, $\text{Budget}_{\max} = 10$

### Padding Generation Rules
The padding set $P$ of size $N_B(L) - k_{\text{sol}}$ is populated using a weighted random distribution based on the unlocked tools pool $U_T$ for the current Tier $T$:
1. **New Tool Presence Guarantee (Tiers 2–7)**: If the introduced tool $t_{\text{new}}$ of Tier $T$ has a count of $0$ in the solution set $S$, there is a **60% probability** that $1$ unit of $t_{\text{new}}$ is added to the padding set $P$. This ensures players cannot easily rule out the new mechanic by examining the briefcase.
2. **Remaining Slots Distribution**:
   - **Mirror**: 40% weight (fundamental routing utility)
   - **Prism / Splitter**: 20% weight (creates alternate paths)
   - **Bender** (Tier 5+): 15% weight
   - **Filter** (Tier 3+): 10% weight
   - **Portal** (Tier 6+): 10% weight
   - **Combiner** (Tier 7+): 5% weight
3. **Mastery Tiers (Tiers 8–10)**: Padding is selected with equal probability across all unlocked tools, but capped at a maximum of $2$ units of any non-Mirror tool to prevent UI overcrowding.

## 8. Word Difficulty & Vocabulary Constraints

The difficulty of the word spelling mechanic is strictly bound to the physical routing constraints of the grid. Because lighting up $N_{\text{unique}}$ letter glyphs simultaneously from a single emitter requires at least $N_{\text{unique}} - 1$ beam splits, the allowed vocabulary is bounded by both word length and unique letter count.

### Unique Letter Caps & Pools by Tier
1. **Duplicate Letter Consolidation**: The board contains exactly one physical Glyph for each unique letter in the word. The UI displays the full word, and activating the Glyph illuminates all instances of that letter.
2. **Tier Vocabulary Parameters**:
   - **Tier 2 (Levels 16-20)**: Length 3-4 letters (Max 3 unique letters). Character Pool: `S, U, N, R, A, Y, O, B, K, D, I, M, L, T, G, W, E` (17 characters).
   - **Tier 3 (Spectrum)**: Length 4 letters (Max 4 unique letters). Character Pool: Tier 2 + `H, Z` (19 characters).
   - **Tier 4 (Shadow)**: Length 4-5 letters (Max 4 unique letters). Character Pool: Tier 3 + `P, C, F` (22 characters).
   - **Tier 5 (Geometry)**: Length 5 letters (Max 5 unique letters). Character Pool: Tier 4 + `J, X` (24 characters).
   - **Tier 6 (Warp)**: Length 5-6 letters (Max 5 unique letters). Character Pool: Full English alphabet.
   - **Tier 7 (Convergence)**: Length 6 letters (Max 5 unique letters). *Note: Combiner activation requires splitting light into two inputs, reducing the budget available for unique letter routing.*
   - **Tier 8 (Mastery)**: Length 6-7 letters (Max 6 unique letters).
   - **Tier 9 (Cipher)**: Length 7 letters (Max 6 unique letters).
   - **Tier 10 (Solstice)**: Length 7-8 letters (Max 6 unique letters).
   - *Exclusion Rule*: Highly unique 8-letter words (e.g., "SPECTRUM", 8 unique letters) are excluded, as routing to 8 targets requires 7 splits, which exceeds the max briefcase budget.

## 9. BackwardsSolver Validation Rules

To prevent generator timeouts and ensure optimal performance on low-end and mobile devices, the procedural generator runs within strict computational limits.

### Computational Safety Budgets
- **Maximum Generator Iterations**: The level scaffolder is capped at **200 layout attempts** per level. If no valid solvable configuration is found within 200 iterations, the scaffolder selects a different target word or reduces the winding coefficient constraint.
- **Pathfinder Search Limit**: The pathfinder is capped at a maximum of **10,000 explored nodes** per single glyph-to-source route search.

### A* Pathfinding Heuristic & Search Costs
The pathfinder's state space represents dynamic vectors `(x, y, dx, dy)` (supporting continuous placement validation off-grid centers). The heuristic function $h(P)$ is standard and admissible:
$$h(P) = \text{EuclideanDistance}(P, P_{\text{target}})$$
To guide the search, the transition step-cost $g(P, P')$ enforces 15-degree snap angles rather than the heuristic, avoiding heuristic dominance and local minima lockups:
$$g(P, P') = \text{StepDistance}(P, P') + f_{\text{align}}(P')$$
where the penalty $f_{\text{align}}(P') = 0$ if the ray direction aligns with a 15-degree multiple, and $\infty$ (representing a blocked transition) otherwise.

### Memory Constraints
- **State Space Cache**: Visited grid configurations and path states are cached. The state cache is capped at **50,000 entries** (approx. 16MB) per level generation.
- **De-allocation**: All temporary search maps and node caches are explicitly cleared at the end of each generation cycle to prevent memory leaks in WebGL and mobile builds.

## 10. Progression & Unlock Economy

The meta-progression uses a non-punishing gating system, allowing players to skip a subset of challenging levels in a tier by accumulating **Energy Nodes** ($\mathcal{E}$) from successfully decrypted words.

### Energy Node Generation Formula
Solving a level awards $\mathcal{E}$ based on word complexity and the active tier:
$$\mathcal{E} = 10 \times N_{\text{unique}} \times M_T$$
where:
- $N_{\text{unique}}$ is the count of unique letters in the decrypted word.
- $M_T$ is the Tier Multiplier: $M_T = 1.0 + (T - 1) \times 0.2$
- **Stardust Bonus**: Sweeping all optional *Stardust Clusters* with an active light beam grants a flat **+20% bonus** to the level's total energy payout.

### Cumulative Unlock Calibration
To unlock Tier $T$ (where $T \ge 2$), players must accumulate a lifetime cumulative sum of Energy Nodes. Unlock thresholds are set to exactly **70% of the maximum possible cumulative energy** earned up through the previous tier (assuming average unique letter counts per level, without optional stardust bonuses):
- **Tier 2 (Refraction)**: $100 \mathcal{E}$ (requires completing ~7 levels in Tier 1)
- **Tier 3 (Spectrum)**: $300 \mathcal{E}$ (requires ~70% cumulative completion of T1-T2)
- **Tier 4 (Shadow)**: $650 \mathcal{E}$ (requires ~70% cumulative completion of T1-T3)
- **Tier 5 (Geometry)**: $1050 \mathcal{E}$ (requires ~70% cumulative completion of T1-T4)
- **Tier 6 (Warp)**: $1650 \mathcal{E}$ (requires ~70% cumulative completion of T1-T5)
- **Tier 7 (Convergence)**: $2300 \mathcal{E}$ (requires ~70% cumulative completion of T1-T6)
- **Tier 8 (Mastery)**: $3000 \mathcal{E}$ (requires ~70% cumulative completion of T1-T7)
- **Tier 9 (Cipher)**: $3900 \mathcal{E}$ (requires ~70% cumulative completion of T1-T8)
- **Tier 10 (Solstice)**: $4900 \mathcal{E}$ (requires ~70% cumulative completion of T1-T9)
